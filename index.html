Indian Fighter Aircraft and Missiles

Fighter Aircraft

India operates a range of advanced fighter aircraft for defense and strategic purposes:

  • HAL Tejas: An indigenous light combat aircraft with modern avionics and a top speed of Mach 1.8.
  • Sukhoi Su-30MKI: A versatile multirole fighter with a range of 3,000 km and advanced weaponry.
  • Dassault Rafale: A state-of-the-art multirole fighter jet equipped with cutting-edge radar and weapons systems.
  • MiG-29: A twin-engine aircraft designed for air superiority and ground attack missions.

Missiles

India's missile arsenal includes strategic and tactical systems for various defense requirements:

  • BrahMos: A supersonic cruise missile with a range of 450-600 km and high precision.
  • Agni Series: Ballistic missiles with ranges from 700 km (Agni-I) to 5,000 km (Agni-V) for strategic deterrence.
  • Prithvi: A short-range tactical missile with a range of 350 km for battlefield use.
  • Akash: A medium-range surface-to-air missile capable of engaging multiple aerial targets.
  • Nirbhay: A subsonic cruise missile with a range of up to 1,000 km and stealth capabilities.
